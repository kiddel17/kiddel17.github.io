Convincing marketers with the right analytics of a campaign in VR

A project for the course DH2655 Cooperative IT-design at KTH

About the project

This is a project for the course DH2655 Cooperative IT-design at KTH, in collaboration with a company moving within the market of advertising in virtual reality (VR).

We set out to discover the environment for in-game advertising for VR. Players in VR games devote their entire attention to the games which is a rare opportunity for marketers to come by. The way that the players interact with the in-game ads are however different than with the traditional website ads. The new way of interaction with adds leads to new challenges for marketers.

Process

Step 1

Background research

These new challenges require new tools. Therefore our aim has been to present Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) and concepts that would allow marketers to make use of this environment. We started our project by interviewing and marketers and gaming companies to learn about the current market. The data we gathered has been summarized and used in our work.

Step 2

Prototyping and Innovation discovery

As we had gotten familiar with the environment of digital and in-game advertising we moved to come up with the KPIs and functionality that would help marketers in this setting. To do this we held a design workshop to build prototypes with possible new solutions. The ideas went through a process of feedback and reflection to refine the ideas and build a digital prototype.

Step 3

Testing and conceptualizing

In the last step of the project we moved on to test the digital prototype. The user tests were performed with marketers as they have been our target group throughout the project. The feedback from them and observations of the testers lead to updating of the prototype and on our conclusions on what concepts and KPIs are likely to work for VR in-game ads. The digital prototype and the report were updated with these concepts.
